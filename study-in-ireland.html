Education System

Education System in Ireland

  • First Level - Primary Education : A child starts first grade when they turn five. The child is the center of the primary curriculum in Irish schools.
  • Second Level - Post-Primary Education : The junior cycle, lasting three years, and the senior cycle, lasting two years, comprise post-primary education.
  • Third Level It consists of universities with the authority to award degrees for a range of bachelor's, master's, and PhD programs.

Admission Intakes

Admission Intakes in Ireland

  • Autumn Intake or Semester 1 : Begins in early September and continues until December
  • Spring intake or Semester 2 : Begins in late January and continues until May
Grading System

Grading System in Ireland

Most courses in Ireland have minimum passing grades of at least 40% of the maximum score. Grading policies vary per institution

  • First Class Honours - 70% or above - A (Excellent)
  • Upper Second Class Honours - 60 - 69% - B (Very good, with few errors)
  • Lower Second Class Honours - 50 - 59% - C (Good, with some errors)
  • Third Class Honours - 45 - 50% - D (Satisfactory, with many errors)
  • Fail - 40 - 45% - E (Sufficient)
