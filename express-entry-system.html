Canada’s Express Entry System

Express Entry has been electronically accepting, managing and processing PR applications from skilled foreign nations under the following economic immigration programs. Express Entry is known as one of the fastest ways to immigrate to Canada as the processing can be as fast as six to seven months and it is one of Canada’s more popular immigration programs. Many foreign nationals favour this program as it is online which makes applying anywhere in the world possible and applicants do not require a job offer. Federal Skilled Trades

The Federal Skilled Trades Program is for skilled workers who want to become permanent residents based on being qualified in a skilled trade.

To be eligible you must have -
  • Minimum Language CLB 5
  • 2 years of full-time work Experience in a Skilled Trade.
  • 1 year full-time job offer from Canada or Certification of Qualification in the Skilled Trade
  • Issue by A Canadian Provincial, Territoral or Federal Authority

Canadian Experience Class

CEC is managed under express entry system and uses the CRS to rank interested candidates. Applicants who have competitive CRS scores will receive ITA for Canadian PR

In order to count your 12-months of Canadian work experience towards your CEC application, it must meet the following conditions:
  • Be obtained in Canada within the 36 months prior to the date of submission of the electronic Application for Permanent Residence (eAPR)
  • Your employment duties in Canada fall under the NOC Code Skill Level 0, A, or B.
  • Be lawfully obtained, paid work experience in Canada.

RIGHT TO LIVE AND WORK

Permanent Residents in Canada are authorized to live and work in any region of the country, subject to any exceptions.

ACCESS TO SOCIAL BENEFITS

Permanent Residents' children receive free education, and immediate family members are eligible for state healthcare.

FAMILY RELOCATION

Permanent Residents are allowed to move with their entire family, including their children and grandparents, to any location.

ELIGIBILITY FOR CITIZENSHIP

After residing as a Permanent Resident for three years, you may apply for Canadian citizenship.
